Entrainment vs. Melodic Distraction

Why avoid layering piano or melodic content over binaural beats? Musical elements evoke emotion and compete for attention within the auditory cortex, creating cognitive interference. Research shows that for neural entrainment to occur, the auditory signal must remain clean and unambiguous (Thut et al., 2012).

The 320kbps Standard

Binaural beats rely on precise phase differences between stereo channels. Lossy compression, such as 128 kbps MP3, distorts these phase relationships, introducing micro-errors that the nervous system interprets as noise rather than coherent rhythm.
