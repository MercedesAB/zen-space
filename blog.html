Entrainment vs. Melodic Distraction

Feb 10, 2026 • Neuroscience

One common misconception in neuroacoustic design is that overlaying music—such as piano or soft melodies—enhances the effect of binaural beats. In reality, the auditory cortex prioritizes complex harmonic structures over rhythmic pulses. By removing melodic information and focusing on pure frequency architecture, we allow the brain to synchronize (entrain) more effectively without competing signals.

The 320kbps Standard

Jan 28, 2026 • Engineering

Binaural beats rely on microphase differences between stereo channels. Standard compressed audio formats, such as 128 kbps MP3s, disrupt these microphase relationships. To ensure the fidelity required for neural entrainment, all ZENSPACE protocols are rendered at 320kbps CBR (Constant Bitrate), preserving the phase integrity essential for the intended cognitive effect.
